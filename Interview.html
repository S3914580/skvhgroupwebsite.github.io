IT Professional

As I mentioned above. At my current job, I am working at COTAI - Center of Talent in AI. In the morning I will do programming and projects for the company. In the company, I have another job that is tutoring. In the evening, I will teach if there is a class. In addition, I teach students at Le Hong Phong High School about Python and AI.

Next, we go to the second question. Can tell us about the industry you work in.

IT Professional

Currently, in my opinion, when working in the programmer industry, there will be 3 areas of work. The first is outsourcing, making products for the company and finally giving extra tutoring on programming subjects. Therefore, I have a question: do you guys know what outsourcing is and what is a product in house?

Vy Bui

Oh sorry, I don’t know much about it. Can you talk more about it?

IT Professional

Outsourcing means that the customer will approach his company and place an order. Maybe the customer will already have ideas and details, I just need to follow. On the other hand, the client will have an idea but they need to listen to advice and need someone with expertise to come up with the idea for them. With planning, I will plan for them, then explain what my solution to their problem is. In the past, there was a time when I worked at FPT, FPT was an outsourcing company and here there were many types of customers. However, often when customers approach large companies, they already know what they want, so there are a few projects they will provide details and I just need to follow. My current company is COTAI with a smaller scale so sometimes I have to plan some projects for them because the client doesn't know it well. Because my company specializes in AI, they want to test whether their idea can be applied to AI or if the customer already has a system, how to apply AI to that system.

Son Vu

So, between those to IT work, which one you prefer more and why?

IT Professional

In my opinion, if you are a new graduate, outsourcing will be better because when you do outsource, you will have access to the details and designs of the customer. In a word, what classes and functions and variables will be in that software, etc. If you have already learned about ADI, what ADIs the system will include and what components will ADI have in it or how to call each other, the customer will provide enough about those parts. When you first get out of school, you will learn more when you approach those things. And if I join a start-up company that makes products, I have to take care of that product, that is in the case of small companies. When I make a product like that, I have to be more creative on my own. For example, in the past, I worked at a start-up company and there were only 3 programmers at that time, the 3 of us had to discuss with each other how to design the system because their superiors only worked in economics and marketing, they don't have IT expertise, so 3 people have to do everything by themselves. As I see it, when you do it yourself, it is possible that with a new graduate level that is not enough, it is easy to get lost and don't know if you are doing it right or wrong. Therefore, when you just graduated from school, you should do outsourcing at a big company, when you have enough experience, you should move to a small company so that you can master the technology you code.

Vy Bui

I know what you mean. This is really good advice for us. For the next question, can you tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals.

IT Professional

At work, I will have interactions with superiors such as team leader and project manager. Every morning in the company, there will be a sign-up meeting of about 5-10 minutes to report to your superiors about what you will do today and what you did yesterday to the team leader so that they can understand the progress. work level. When you become a team leader or have been working for a while, you will report directly to the person in charge of the project. If they choose to work at a smaller company, they will be able to interact directly with the board of directors about their ideas. For example, when I run a start-up, I get to pitch my ideas for improvements and features that user might like. At that time, you will be able to contribute to the requirements of that project, not just the code. Obviously, the latter will be more interesting because the children will be able to contribute their ideas to the product, rather than following someone else's request.

Son Vu

Oh, actually in my daily work, interacting with colleagues will often happen when I have difficulty or something I don't understand. Vy Bui

We can see lots of work you have to do in IT work so what aspects of your work do you spend most time on? Please tell us about these.

IT Professional

People often say "think first, code later". First, I will read a requirement file to get an idea. Brainstorming usually takes a lot of time. For example, if the boss wants to have an interface that requires different customizations, after I hear the idea, I will look at the design and then think if I want to do so, what class should I write and what are the properties inside that class. Also must determine how to design the API. In addition, I also have to pay attention to whether my current database can contain this information, or whether additional tables/columns are needed. If I read the requirements in the morning, then I don't start coding until noon or afternoon. When I think carefully, the code will be very fast. And again, when I receive a task from a manager or a client when they want to do something, I quickly think about whether my current system can do that. But it's not like I'll agree to everything they say and then when I check again and find I can't do it and report back, that won't be good.

Son Vu

I agree with you with what you share with us. As you said before we meet so many requirements during the time we work. Do you have any advice that you can suggest to us what we need to prepare in the time we’re studying in university?

IT Professional

I have a question back for you guys. Have you guys made any group coding products yet?

Son Vu

I and 3 other group members wrote code together and we had to make a plan and make a schedule and did it very soon to complete it before the deadline. We did everything together.

IT Professional

It is true that in school will teach you to plan, schedule, etc, in general, things related to management. When fresh out of school, the task will be more about coding. As I said when you read that requirement, in your mind you will need to think of what class and how to design. Therefore, to make these things simpler, students should focus on subjects such as data structures, algorithms, and databases. Besides, you can see more design pattern courses online. In my opinion, the above 3 things are the most important because during the interview, you will encounter a lot of questions about the above contents, partly because the above 3 contents are very important when working on a project. Talking a bit about the Design pattern, then the Design Pattern has 2 areas, the first is the folder tree in the project, I have to create a folder. For example, you will create a folder by function, this folder will contain everything related to management because in the system there is customer management, user management, product management in the website or product interaction management. On the other hand, the simplest one is an MVC pattern, which is the most basic design pattern. You can find out more on the internet. It will have classes like the user class, what information the product has, a view model and a controller. First, there will be classes and controllers - where the logic is handled - for example, if you want to create a new user in the system, the user will enter the information and password, the controller will only handle the logic, it will receive the username. Enter and password and check if this username exists or if the password is valid. Then we will create a user object and put the user object into a storage place like a database or excel. It will have 3 individual entries. If I say more, it may cause some confusion for you, so after this interview, you can read more online and you will see that the activities of these 3 are not related to each other. You can learn more about the design pattern with the most basic being the MVC pattern.

Once upon a time, I worked at a start-up called go 2 joy. What I do is about booking hotels like Traveloka or Agoda. When I started working on it, the project was in progress. Many people say that doing it is dry because sitting in front of the computer typing commands, but when doing it, it is much more fun. When doing so, they will have access to many other jobs like when I do, I know more about marketing: how to attract users when my app is just posted on the Appstore and how to create promotions. I learned how to make a complete project from the leader of that project. The most fun is when it's done and the product is in shape, then I see how other people use it and get feedback. When doing a big project, there will be tracing and monitoring, then I will open it to see how many users used my app yesterday or what district the users came from. Watching those things is also fun, like when I open the app at the weekend, I see other people using my app a lot. I'm so happy because I feel like it's my own child.
